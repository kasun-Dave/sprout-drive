You are an expert full-stack developer. Build a simple, cloud-based web application for a medium-scale mung bean sprout company.

Business summary:
- The company buys mung beans (and some other plant items like broccoli) from suppliers.
- They plant mung beans on “beds” (planting trays/plates).
- Mung sprouts are ready on day 6.
- Delivery people come daily, take ready sprouts, put them in water, then bag them, and deliver to customers.
- Owner manually calls customers, gets the required quantity, tells the delivery driver, and later manually enters sales into Excel.
- I want to replace this with one web system.

## Main requirements

### 1. User roles & login
- Role 1: Owner/Admin
  - Can see everything: suppliers, planting, stock, customers, orders, vans, reports.
- Role 2: Staff/Delivery
  - Can see only what they need: today’s delivery list, quantities to bag, and a simple screen to enter delivered quantities and cash collected.
- Simple email/password login is enough.

---

### 2. Suppliers & purchasing
- Manage supplier list:
  - Supplier name
  - Phone, address
  - Type of items supplied (mung beans, broccoli, other vegetables, etc.)
- Record purchases from suppliers:
  - Date
  - Supplier
  - Item (e.g., mung beans, broccoli, etc.)
  - Quantity (kg)
  - Price per kg, total price
- Show current stock for:
  - Mung beans (raw)
  - Other items (direct resale items like broccoli)

---

### 3. Planting & 6-day sprout planning
For mung beans only, we need a 6-day planting and harvest plan.

- A “bed” (plant plate) is the unit of planting.
- When the owner plants, they should be able to create a “Planting Batch”:
  - Date of planting
  - Number of beds
  - Beans used (kg)
  - Expected ready date = planting date + 6 days (auto-calculated)
- The system should estimate how many kg of sprouts will be available on the ready date based on a simple conversion ratio that I can change (for example: 1 kg beans → 6 kg sprouts). Keep this ratio configurable.

- Provide a “Planning” view for the owner:
  - For each day (next 7–10 days), show:
    - How many beds will be ready
    - Estimated available sprouts (kg)
  - Allow the owner to decide how many beds to plant today so that in 6 days there is enough stock for the regular customers.

---

### 4. Customers & daily orders
- Manage customers:
  - Customer name
  - Shop name (if different)
  - Phone
  - Address
  - Delivery route / delivery van (optional)
  - Usual daily quantity (optional, e.g., “usually 5 kg”)

- Daily orders:
  - Owner can create/edit an “Order” for each customer for a specific date:
    - Date
    - Customer
    - Product (mung sprouts or any other item)
    - Ordered quantity (kg or units)
  - There should be a “Today’s Orders” overview screen:
    - List all customers for a selected date (default: today)
    - Show ordered quantity for each customer per product.

---

### 5. Bagging, delivery & quantity adjustments
Delivery staff process:

- For each day (e.g., today):
  - See “Ready sprouts” based on planting (total available quantity).
  - See “Today’s Orders” list for mung sprouts (per customer).

- They should have a simple screen:
  - For each customer and product:
    - Ordered quantity
    - “Bagged quantity” (what they actually bag)
    - “Delivered quantity” (what actually reached customer)
  - Example:
    - Bagged: 300 kg
    - Delivered: 250 kg
    - Remaining: 50 kg → this should go back into stock/available quantity.

- After delivery:
  - Mark order as:
    - Delivered
    - Cancelled (if needed)
  - For each order, store:
    - Cash collected
    - Payment type (cash / credit / other)
    - Invoice number

- System must automatically:
  - Reduce stock by delivered quantity.
  - Keep track of any leftover sprouts from “bagged – delivered” and mark them as leftover/returned.

---

### 6. Billing & reports
- Invoice generation:
  - For each delivered order, generate a simple invoice:
    - Company name and logo (placeholders)
    - Customer name
    - Date
    - Items (product, quantity, price per unit, total)
    - Grand total
  - PDF or printable HTML is enough.

- Reports for owner:
  - Daily sales report:
    - Total sales by date
    - Breakdown by customer and product
  - Stock summary:
    - Current mung bean raw stock
    - Current sprouts ready stock (today and coming days)
    - Stock of other items.
  - Planting summary:
    - Beds planted, beans used, expected harvest.
  - Customer summary:
    - How much each customer bought in a date range.

---

### 7. Vans / delivery vehicles management
- Manage list of delivery vans:
  - Vehicle number
  - Model
  - Insurance expiry date
  - Revenue licence expiry date
  - Notes.

- Maintenance log:
  - Date
  - Van
  - Type of service/repair
  - Cost
  - Notes.

- Reminders:
  - Show a warning on the dashboard when:
    - Insurance expiry or revenue licence expiry is within 30 days.
    - Upcoming service based on last service date (e.g., every 6 months, keep this interval configurable).

---

### 8. UX & technology
- Make it a simple, mobile-friendly web app (responsive).
- Clean, easy UI because users are not very technical.
- Use a common modern stack (for example: React + Node.js/Express + PostgreSQL/MySQL), or any full-stack framework you are comfortable with.
- Include basic data validation and error messages.
- Add seed/sample data so I can see a working example (a few suppliers, customers, vans, planting batches, and orders).

---

### 9. Nice-to-have (if easy)
- A dashboard homepage for the owner that shows:
  - Today’s total ready sprouts.
  - Today’s total ordered quantity vs. stock.
  - Number of deliveries today.
  - Upcoming van insurance/licence expiries.

Build this as a complete project with:
- Backend API
- Database schema and migrations
- Frontend UI
- Instructions on how to run the project locally and online (deploy on Replit).
