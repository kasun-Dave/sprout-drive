### 10. Analytics, charts & demand predictions

Add an Analytics module for the Owner/Admin with:

#### 10.1 Past orders overview (charts)

Create a visual analytics page with clear charts (use any chart library you like, e.g., Chart.js):

From the orders, show at least:

- Sales over time:
  - Line chart: total sales per day / per week / per month.
- Product-wise breakdown:
  - Bar or pie chart: total quantity sold per product (mung sprouts, broccoli, etc.) in a selected date range.
- Customer-wise breakdown:
  - Bar chart: top customers by quantity or value in a selected period.
- Seasonal patterns:
  - For each month of the year, show:
    - Total quantity of mung sprouts sold.
    - Total quantity of other products.
  - This should help see high and low demand months.

Filters:
- Date range filter (from–to).
- Filter by product.
- Filter by customer (optional).

#### 10.2 Planting & purchase predictions

Using past order data, add a simple prediction/planning helper:

- For mung sprouts:
  - For a selected future month or upcoming 4–6 weeks:
    - Estimate expected demand per day and per week.
    - Suggest:
      - Average kg of sprouts needed per day.
      - Based on the beans→sprouts ratio and beds capacity:
        - How many kg of beans should be planted per day.
        - How many beds should be planted per day (approx).
  - Use a simple forecasting approach:
    - Start with moving average of the last few weeks/months.
    - If possible, give more weight to recent data.
    - It doesn’t need to be perfect machine learning; a simple, understandable logic is fine.

- For other items (like broccoli and direct-supplier items):
  - For each product, suggest:
    - Average quantity to purchase per week/month based on past sales in that same season or month.
  - Example:
    - “In the last 3 months, this customer/product used around X kg per week.”

Outputs:
- A “Planning suggestion” section that clearly shows:
  - For the selected period:
    - Suggested daily/weekly planting (beds & kg beans).
    - Suggested weekly/monthly purchase quantity per product from suppliers.
- Show these suggestions in:
  - Simple text cards, and
  - At least one supporting chart (e.g., bar chart comparing historical and suggested future quantities).

#### 10.3 UX

- Keep the Analytics page very simple and readable:
  - Big titles, clear labels, tooltips on charts.
- Allow exporting key analytics views (for example, CSV export for summary tables, if easy).
